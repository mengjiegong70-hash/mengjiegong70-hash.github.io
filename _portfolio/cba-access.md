## Overview

**CBA-Access** is a Python toolkit for evaluating spatial accessibility to electric vehicle charging facilities. It is designed to support reproducible analysis of charging pile accessibility, supply-demand matching, and GIS-based visualization in urban environments.

This tool was developed in the context of charging pile accessibility research, aiming to make accessibility computation more standardized, reusable, and easier to apply in different cities or study areas.

## Why This Tool?

With the rapid growth of electric vehicles, the spatial distribution of charging facilities has become an important issue in urban planning and transportation geography. However, charging piles are often unevenly distributed, and their accessibility may vary across neighborhoods, travel modes, time periods, and demand groups.

CBA-Access provides a computational workflow for asking questions such as:

- Which areas have insufficient access to charging facilities?
- How well does charging supply match potential EV demand?
- Where are the spatial gaps between residential demand and charging infrastructure?
- How can accessibility indicators support charging facility planning?
