I am a master’s student in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) at Sun Yat-sen University. My research focuses on **Spatial Accessibility Modeling**, **Causal Inference**, and **Environmental Health Exposure Analysis**.

## Research Interests

- **Spatial Accessibility Modeling**: Developing novel computational methods and tools for quantifying spatial accessibility, with applications spanning healthcare facilities, grocery stores, urban green spaces, and accessibility dynamics under extreme weather events.

- **Causal Inference**: Developing causal inference methods tailored for spatial data, with broad applications in urban, environmental, and public health contexts.

- **Environmental Health Exposure Analysis**: Developing dynamic trajectory-based exposure assessment methods, with applications in sentiment exposure, thermal exposure, street-level built environment exposure, and noise exposure.

## Current Research

My current work explores how spatial data, remote sensing, urban big data, and computational modeling can be used to understand urban environments and support evidence-based planning.

I am particularly interested in questions such as:

- How can we measure spatial accessibility more accurately across space and time?
- How do urban resources, such as elderly care services, healthcare facilities, grocery stores, and green spaces, match population demand?
- How can causal inference be adapted to spatial and spatiotemporal data?
- How do dynamic human mobility patterns shape environmental exposure and health-related risks?

Beyond formal research projects, I am also interested in geospatial visualization, story maps, urban perception analysis, social media data mining, and public review data analysis.
